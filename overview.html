OVERVIEW

Identifying individuals living below the poverty line (BPL) in rural areas is a fundamental step in ensuring equitable distribution of resources and implementing targeted development interventions. By employing a combination of traditional criteria, participatory methods, and technological innovations, stakeholders strive to accurately identify and assist those most in need within rural communities. This process is pivotal in addressing the multidimensional challenges of poverty and fostering inclusive growth in rural regions.
